inverse_taylor¶

mpsci.polyapprox.inverse_taylor(f, x0, n)¶

Taylor polynomial coefficients of the inverse of \(f\).

Given a callable \(f\), and a point \(x_0\), find the Taylor polynomial of degree n of the inverse of \(f\) at \(x_0\).

If \(y_0\) = \(f(x_0)\), and if the inverse of \(f\) is \(g\), this function returns the Taylor polynomial coefficients of \(g(y)\) at \(y_0\).

\(f'(x_0)\) must be nonzero.

Examples

>>> import mpmath
>>> mpmath.mp.dps = 40

Compute the Taylor coefficients of the inverse of the sine function sin(x) at x=1.

>>> inverse_taylor(mpmath.sin, 1, 5)
[mpf('1.0'),
 mpf('1.850815717680925617911753241398650193470396'),
 mpf('2.667464736243829370645086306803786566557799'),
 mpf('8.745566949501434796799480049601499630239969'),
 mpf('34.55691117453807764026147509020588920253199'),
 mpf('152.9343377104818039879748855586655382173672')]

Compare that to computing the Taylor polynomial coefficients of the arcsine function directly:

>>> mpmath.taylor(mpmath.asin, mpmath.sin(1), 5)
[mpf('1.0'),
 mpf('1.850815717680925617911753241398650193470396'),
 mpf('2.667464736243829370645086306803786566557799'),
 mpf('8.745566949501434796799480049601499630240153'),
 mpf('34.55691117453807764026147509020588920253199'),
 mpf('152.9343377104818039879748855586655382173702')]
